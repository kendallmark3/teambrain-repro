Most AI Tools
  • ✗
    Jump straight to solutions

  • ✗
    Invent requirements

    Miss enterprise constraints

    Optimize for speed over correctness

Non-Negotiables

6 Core Principles

TeamBrain enforces these without exception. Skip one and you've built on a hidden assumption.

01

Reasoning Before Solutions

No solution is proposed until the problem is fully restated, constraints are surfaced, and the solution space is understood. The fastest path to a wrong answer is skipping this step.

02

Explicit Assumptions

Every assumption is named out loud. Hidden assumptions are hidden failures waiting for the right stress condition. If you're taking something for granted, say it — so it can be challenged before it becomes a production incident.

03

Clear Tradeoffs

Every decision has a cost. Speed vs. reliability. Simplicity vs. flexibility. Centralized vs. distributed. Name what you're gaining and what you're giving up — before the decision is locked in and the cost of reversing it is six weeks.

04

Risk Surfaced Early

The top failure modes are named before building starts — not discovered in a post-mortem. The cost of surfacing a risk early is a conversation. The cost of discovering it late is a production incident with an SLA review attached.

05

No Invented Facts

If something is unclear, TeamBrain calls it out instead of guessing. Fabricated confidence is more dangerous than admitted uncertainty. An AI that stops and asks is more valuable than one that fills the gap with a plausible invention.

06

No Hidden Requirements

Every constraint is made visible. Security policies, compliance rules, org structure, budget limits, team skill gaps — if it affects the decision, it belongs in the room. Silent constraints are the most dangerous kind.

💬
If something is unclear, TeamBrain calls it out instead of guessing.

This is not a limitation — it's the most important behavior in the framework. Acknowledged uncertainty is a decision input. Fabricated confidence is a failure mode.

Getting Started

No Setup. No Dependencies. No Permissions Required.

Open a markdown file. Paste it into your AI tool. Provide real context. Let TeamBrain do the rest.

1

Open Any Markdown File in the Repo

Start with the core prompts in /teambrain/TeamBrain.md for the reasoning framework, or /intent/architecture.intent.md if you're adopting the full framework for a project team.

$ git clone github.com/kendallmark3/TeamBrain
$ open teambrain/TeamBrain.md
2

Paste Into Your AI Tool of Choice

TeamBrain is tool-agnostic. Paste the prompt content into ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, Gemini, or any AI that accepts system context. The framework works wherever you already work.

ChatGPTClaudeCopilotGeminiAny AI
3

Provide Your Real Context

Don't use generic examples. Provide your actual decision: the real system, the real constraints, the real team, the real org. TeamBrain's value comes from applying it to your actual situation — not hypotheticals.

Instead of:
"How should I design a microservices system?"
Provide:
"We have a 12-year-old monolith, a 6-person team, no Kubernetes experience, and leadership is asking us to decompose it. Here are our constraints..."
4

Let TeamBrain Review, Challenge, and Clarify

TeamBrain will restate the problem, surface assumptions, name tradeoffs, flag risks, and — if something is unclear — stop and ask rather than invent. That last behavior is the most important one.

✅ Problem restated before any solution is proposed
✅ Assumptions named and challengeable
✅ At least two approaches with explicit tradeoffs
✅ Top risks flagged before you start building
✅ Unclear requirements trigger a stop, not an invention
